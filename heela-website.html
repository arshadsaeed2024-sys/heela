The problem

The gap isn't ability.
It's navigation.

Refugee students in the United States face compounding barriers to college access. They navigate unfamiliar educational systems with limited institutional knowledge, often without parents who can guide them through applications, financial aid, or standardized testing.

School counselors are stretched thin and rarely equipped to address the specific needs of displaced youth. The result is not a lack of ambition. It's a lack of structured, sustained support at the exact moment it matters most.

Families unfamiliar with U.S. higher education systems have no roadmap to follow.

Overburdened school counselors lack the capacity for refugee-specific guidance.

Fragmented support programs offer short-term help, not sustained follow-through.

Transportation, language, and cultural barriers block access to what help does exist.

"This is a navigation problem, not a capability problem."
How we work

Two people. One student.
Forty weeks.

Every student is paired with a trained, paid Student Fellow — a university student who provides weekly one-on-one guidance through the entire college preparation process, from building a list to enrollment day.

Student Fellow

A paid, near-peer mentor from a U.S. university. W-2 employee, not a volunteer. Capped at five students to protect the depth that makes this work. Weekly Zoom sessions, 45 to 60 minutes.

Executive Director

Supervises every Fellow. Monitors every student's progress. Holds safeguarding responsibility. When something goes off track, the ED intervenes — not after the deadline passes, but before.

What makes Heela different

Not another mentoring program.

01

Structure is care

Every session follows a clear format. Every student has an individualized college prep plan with milestones and deadlines. Progress is tracked weekly — not loosely hoped for.

02

Accountability both ways

We ask students to show up and do the work. In return, we commit to being prepared, responsive, and honest. The system catches problems early, not after it's too late.

03

Near-peer relationships

Fellows are close enough in age and experience to be credible guides — not distant authority figures. The relationship is the method, and it only works when it's real.

04

Ethical by design

We serve minors across cultures and power imbalances. Safeguarding, boundaries, and cultural humility are built into every process — not added as afterthoughts.

05

Through enrollment

Heela doesn't end when the application is submitted. We walk with students through financial aid, decision-making, enrollment, and the transition to campus.

06

Context, not identity

Our students are refugees. That matters for understanding their barriers. It does not define their potential. This is not a pity program — it's a navigation program.
